How to Use System Design Wizard
Click to expand — 5-step guide + pro tips
▼
1
Select Application Type & Identification
Choose the system type (winch, offshore crane, steering gear, thruster, etc.) and enter your project and vessel name. Select the installation environment and duty cycle — these set the AI context for all downstream sizing decisions.
2
Enter Power Requirements
Specify working pressure (bar), required flow rate (L/min), actuator type, load characteristics, and prime mover rating. Always enter your actual design working pressure — not the component rated pressure. The AI will set relief valves at 110–115% automatically.
3
Select Fluid, Thermal & Circuit Architecture
Choose hydraulic fluid type, ISO cleanliness target, cooler strategy, and circuit architecture (open loop, closed loop, load sensing). Select all safety devices required. For offshore classified systems, select your classification society — DNV, LR, or BV.
4
Review Summary & Generate AI Design
Confirm all parameters in the summary review table, then click "Generate Design." The AI produces a structured 10-section output: circuit architecture, pump/motor sizing, DCV selection, pipe schedule, BOM table, thermal duty, accumulator sizing, and commissioning parameters.
💡 Pro Tips
Enter actual working pressure — not component rated pressure — for accurate relief valve and pipe sizing.
For overrunning loads (gravity-loaded winches, crane luffing), tick Counterbalance Valves — the AI will include CBV setting calculations.
Use the Application Description field for specific requirements (load hold time, fail-safe behaviour, redundancy) — this significantly improves AI design output.
After generation, click Copy to paste the BOM table directly into your procurement or engineering document.
Use synthetic ester fluid (biodegradable) for environmentally sensitive offshore zones — the AI will adjust filtration and sealing recommendations accordingly.
