Describe the Fault Clearly
Enter the crane symptom or fault in plain language (e.g., boom hoist slow on raise, normal on lower). Include which motion is affected and under what load condition.
02
Specify the Crane Model
Mention the manufacturer and model if known (e.g., Liebherr RL-K 600, MacGregor AHC). This helps the AI narrow the hydraulic circuit context.
03
Include Observed Readings
Add any gauge readings, temperatures, noises, or abnormal behaviors you have already noted. The more data you provide, the more precise the diagnosis.
04
Review the Diagnostic Steps
The AI will return a structured fault analysis. Follow the steps in sequence — each step isolates a specific part of the hydraulic circuit.
05
Cross-Reference Your Schematic
Always have the physical hydraulic schematic available. The AI will reference circuit paths, test points, and ISO symbols — verify against your drawing.

Safety Warning
Never perform live pressure testing without proper LOTO isolation. Identify accumulator dump points before opening any circuit.
